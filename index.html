A Wee Bit About Me

I am currently in my Second Year at the University of Glasgow pursuing a major in Electrical and Electronic Engineering with a minor in Music. This might seem like an unusual combination, but both of these subjects allow me to be analytical and creative in equal measure. I like to creatively tackle difficult problems, whether they occur within circuits or symphonies!

I have wanted to fix problems ever since I was a child. Before I even knew what an "engineer" was, I wanted to be an "inventor" or a "fixer". Naturally, when I discovered found out about engineering as a discipline, I was immediately drawn.

My first real taste of engineering was at the 2016 Engineering the Future for Girls programme, which was a great opportunity to engage with a wide range of engineering challenges and see myself as a prospective engineer.

I then participated in work experience at Babcock Rosyth in June 2017, which involved a team project to design a model tower, and present our work. This solidified my interest in engineering and strengthened my teamwork skills.

Later in 2017, I was selected to be the youth reporter for the 2017 Future Asset Conference, an event to encourage young women into the world of investment and asset management. As part of this role, I was given the opportunity to interview Jayne-Anne Gadhia (then CEO of Virgin Money), and Nicola Sturgeon (First Minister of Scotland). Whilst by this point I was leaning towards a future in technology and engineering, this experience opened my eyes to the technology roles available within the finance sector.

In 2018, I was a member of the winning team of Venture Jam, an event that involved teams of young people working together to tackle the world's most pressing challenges. That year, the challenge was to tackle plastic pollution in the events industry. Our team's solution was a plastic jukebox, which combined a reverse vending machine model with a live voting jukebox. This was the first time I was able to see one of my ideas come to life and was a great experience to develop as a budding engineer.

Whilst in school I was also an active member of the Stemettes. Through the Stemettes I attended Monster Confidence, an event to engage young women in STEM through inspirational talks and meetings with people in industry, and AlGirlRhythm, an event to encourage young women into technical, digital, and musical careers.
